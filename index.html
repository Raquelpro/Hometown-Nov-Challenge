Corrientes, Argentina

Corrientes is the capital city of the province of Corrientes, Argentina, located on the eastern shore of the Paraná River, about 1,000 km from Buenos Aires and 300 km from Posadas, on National Route 12. It has a population of 346,334 according to the 2010 Census.

Turist Attractions

  • Parque nacional Mburucuyá
    • Mburucuyá National Park is a protected area located in the Mburucuyá department of Corrientes Province, northeastern Argentina.
  • Basílica de Itatí
    • The Basilica of Our Lady of Itatí is a Catholic sanctuary and temple, main center of veneration to the image of the Virgin of Itatí and located in front of the main square of Itatí, province of Corrientes, Argentina.

Fiestas/Parties

  • Fiesta Nacional del Carnaval
    • Carnival is a public celebration that takes place immediately before Christian Lent. Mostly 3 days of carnival are celebrated
  • Fiesta Nacional del Chamamé
    • The Chamamé Festival is celebrated every year for several days in January, the chamamé sounds and is danced.
  • Fiesta Nacional del Surubí
    • Fishermen from all over Argentina as well as from other countries participate in this contest. The teams of registered fishermen depart in the afternoon aboard between 900 and 950 boats, which makes this competition the most important sport fishing tournament in the country
